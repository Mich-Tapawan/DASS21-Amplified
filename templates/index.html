What it is

The Depression, Anxiety and Stress Scale (DASS-21) is a widely used 21-item screening tool. You answer how often each statement applied to you over the past week. Answers are grouped into three subscales— Depression, Anxiety, and Stress—and converted into scores with established severity bands. Learn more in this Verywell Mind overview of DASS-21 or the original DASS-21 questionnaire (PDF).

Validated metrics

Uses the established 21-item DASS instrument with severity bands aligned to the full DASS-42 scale.

Guided digital screening

Complete Depression, Anxiety, and Stress sections at your own pace on desktop or mobile.

What “Amplified” adds

Beyond standard scoring, this app applies machine learning models trained on survey data to estimate likelihood, magnitude, and a projected future depression score—statistical projections for education and research, not a medical opinion.

Analysis

Magnitude of deviation

Estimates how far a depression score may sit above or below the mild cut-off, given anxiety and stress scores.

Prediction

Escalation likelihood

Models the likelihood of surpassing mild depression and a projected future severity band from your current answers.

How it works

  1. Answer 21 items

    Move through Depression, Anxiety, and Stress sections at your own pace.

  2. Submit for scoring

    Subscale totals are multiplied by 2 to align with the full DASS-42 scale, then matched to severity bands.

  3. Read scores & interpretation

    Review findings, open plain-language interpretation, and visit Info for cut-offs and support resources.
