What it is

The Depression, Anxiety and Stress Scale (DASS-21) is a widely used 21-item screening tool. You answer how often each statement applied to you over the past week. Answers are grouped into three subscales— Depression, Anxiety, and Stress—and converted into scores with established severity bands.

What “Amplified” adds

Beyond standard scoring, this app applies machine learning models trained on survey data to estimate:

  • Likelihood of moving above the mild depression threshold, given your anxiety and stress scores
  • Magnitude of deviation from the mild depression cut-off
  • A projected future depression score and severity band

These are statistical projections for education and research—not a forecast of your personal future or a medical opinion.

How it works

  1. 1

    Answer 21 items

    Move through Depression, Anxiety, and Stress sections at your own pace.

  2. 2

    Submit for scoring

    Subscale totals are multiplied by 2 to align with the full DASS-42 scale, then matched to severity bands.

  3. 3

    Read scores & interpretation

    Review findings, open plain-language interpretation, and visit Info for cut-offs and support resources.

This application is for educational and research purposes only. It is not a substitute for professional psychological diagnosis, treatment, or clinical assessment. Always consult a qualified mental health professional for personal advice.
