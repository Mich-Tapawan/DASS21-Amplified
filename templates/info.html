Understanding your DASS-21 scores

The Depression, Anxiety and Stress Scale (DASS-21) uses 21 questions grouped into three subscales. Each item is scored 0–3 (Never to Very Often), then subscale totals are multiplied by 2 to align with the full DASS-42 scale. Your severity band is determined by comparing your score to established cut-offs below.

What severity levels may mean

These descriptions are general guidance for each band. Individual experiences vary; a professional can help interpret results in context.

  • Normal: Scores are within a typical range for the general population on that subscale.
  • Mild: Some symptoms may be present but are often manageable with self-care and lifestyle support.
  • Moderate: Symptoms are more noticeable and may affect daily life; consider speaking with a counselor or GP.
  • Severe: Symptoms are likely interfering with functioning; professional support is recommended.
  • Extremely severe: Symptoms are intense and persistent; seek prompt help from a mental health professional.

Predictive findings (assessment page)

After you submit the questionnaire, additional metrics estimate patterns based on machine learning models trained on survey data:

  • Likelihood of surpassing mild depression: A percentage estimate of whether your depression score may move above the mild threshold, given your current anxiety and stress scores.
  • Magnitude of deviation from mild depression: How far your depression score may sit above or below the mild cut-off (score of 13), in model units.
  • Predicted future depression score & severity: A projected depression score and corresponding severity band. These are statistical projections, not forecasts of your personal future.

Symptoms identifier results

On the Symptoms identifier page, free text is analyzed with natural language processing to match phrases related to DASS-21 items.

  • Symptoms found: DASS-21 questionnaire items grouped by Depression, Anxiety, or Stress, with the phrase that matched shown as evidence. No matches only means your wording did not match known patterns.
  • Findings counts: The number of unique DASS-21 themes matched in each category—not a clinical score.

Self-care and next steps

  • Maintain regular sleep, meals, and gentle physical activity when possible.
  • Stay connected with people you trust; isolation can worsen low mood and anxiety.
  • Limit alcohol and stimulants if they worsen how you feel.
  • Use brief grounding or breathing exercises when anxiety spikes.
  • If scores are mild or above, or symptoms persist for two weeks or more, consider a school counselor, GP, or licensed mental health professional.
  • Keep a simple log of mood and triggers to discuss with a clinician if you seek help.
