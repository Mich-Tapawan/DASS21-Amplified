Clinical disclaimer

This application is for educational and research purposes only. The DASS-21 is a screening tool, not a diagnostic instrument. Results reflect symptom intensity over the past week and statistical models—not a formal diagnosis or medical opinion. Always consult a qualified mental health professional for personal advice.

DASS-21 scoring guide

Each item is scored 0–3 (Never to Very Often). Subscale totals are multiplied by 2 to align with the full DASS-42 scale. References: UNSW DASS-21, original questionnaire (PDF), Verywell Mind overview.

Severity Depression Anxiety Stress
Normal 0–9 0–7 0–14
Mild 10–13 8–9 15–18
Moderate 14–20 10–14 19–25
Severe 21–27 15–19 26–33
Extremely Severe 28+ 20+ 34+

Understanding the tiers

These descriptions are general guidance. Individual experiences vary; a professional can help interpret results in context.

  • Normal

    Scores are within a typical range for the general population on that subscale.

  • Mild

    Some symptoms may be present but are often manageable with self-care and lifestyle support.

  • Moderate

    Symptoms are more noticeable and may affect daily life; consider speaking with a counselor or GP.

  • Severe

    Symptoms are likely interfering with functioning; professional support is recommended.

  • Extremely Severe

    Symptoms are intense and persistent; seek prompt help from a mental health professional.

ML methodology

After you submit the questionnaire on the assessment page, additional metrics estimate patterns using logistic and linear regression models trained on survey data. These are statistical projections for education and research—not forecasts of your personal future.

  • Logistic regression

    Estimates the likelihood of surpassing mild depression, given your anxiety and stress scores (probability of moving above the mild threshold). Background: Logistic regression explained.

  • Linear regression

    Estimates the magnitude of deviation from the mild depression cut-off (score of 13), and supports a projected future depression score and severity band. Background: Linear regression explained.

General wellness tips

  • Maintain regular sleep, meals, and gentle physical activity when possible.
  • Stay connected with people you trust; isolation can worsen low mood and anxiety.
  • Use brief grounding or breathing exercises when anxiety spikes.
  • If scores are mild or above, or symptoms persist for two weeks or more, consider a counselor, GP, or licensed professional.
  • Limit alcohol and stimulants if they worsen how you feel.
  • Keep a simple log of mood and triggers to discuss with a clinician if you seek help.
